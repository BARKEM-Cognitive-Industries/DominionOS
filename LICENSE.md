## FAQ

**Q: I'm a company but this is just internal research. Do I need a commercial license?**  
A: No. Internal use is covered under the research exception. You only need a license if you're selling it or incorporating it into a commercial product.

**Q: Can I use AetherOS code in my closed-source proprietary software?**  
A: Not without a commercial license. AGPLv3 requires sharing improvements.

**Q: What if I modify AetherOS but don't distribute it?**  
A: You can modify it freely. You only need to share improvements if you distribute it (AGPLv3 §4-5).

**Q: Do I have to open-source my entire product if I use AetherOS?**  
A: Under AGPLv3, you have to share modifications to AetherOS itself and provide access to the source. The rest of your product can stay proprietary. (See AGPLv3 §13 for the AGPL-specific requirements.)

**Q: What's the pricing for a commercial license?**  
A: We discuss it case-by-case based on your use. Email contact@cognitive-industries.org.

**Q: Can I fork AetherOS and sell my fork?**  
A: Not with AGPLv3 code. You'd need to get a commercial license first, then negotiate custom terms.
